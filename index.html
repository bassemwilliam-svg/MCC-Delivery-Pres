The Critical Difference

Performance tells you what happened.
Capability tells you what will happen next.

Think of it like a medical exam. A blood test shows your numbers today. But it doesn't tell you how your body responds to stress, infection, or crisis. Capability is the immune system. Performance is just the snapshot.

Performance Scoring

  • Did they detect the threat? Yes or no.
  • How fast did they respond?
  • How many false alarms?
  • Final score: 82. "Competent."
  • No context. No prediction. Conversation over.

Capability Measurement

  • Can they read a situation under real stress?
  • Do they collapse or recover after errors?
  • How does their attention shift when heart rate spikes?
  • Are they getting stronger or decaying between sessions?
  • What specific intervention will close their gap?
Two operators both score 82. One recovers from mistakes in 20 seconds. The other spirals for 90. A performance score treats them as equal. Capability measurement knows they are fundamentally different.

Grounded in Neuroscience and Human Factors Research

  • Ericsson (2008) - Deliberate practice vs innate ability in expert performance
  • Diamond (2013) - Executive functions and cognitive performance under stress
  • Hancock & Warm (2019) - Vigilance and sustained attention in monitoring tasks
  • Wickens et al. (2015) - Workload, stress, and operator performance degradation
  • Kahneman (2011) - System 1 vs System 2 decision making under pressure
  • Arnsten (2009) - Stress-induced prefrontal cortex dysfunction in decision making

Standards Compliance Intelligence

Your standards.
Our intelligence.

Your surveillance framework defines 4 coverage levels. We measure whether your operators actually deliver them under pressure.

Identification Level
500 px/m | 25 fps
"Can the operator identify an unknown person at the entrance?"

Entrances, exits, control rooms. CAPS validates whether the operator's attention meets this standard when it matters most.

Recognition Level
125 px/m | 12 fps
"Can the operator distinguish a known person in the lobby?"

Lobbies, cash areas, corridors. Sustained attention across multiple feeds at recognition-grade density.

Detection Level
30 px/m | 12 fps
"Can the operator detect movement at the perimeter?"

Perimeters, parking, landscape. The minimum standard. Below this under stress, operators are functionally blind.

Vehicle View Level
500 px/m | 25 fps
"Can the operator read the plate at the gate?"

Vehicle entrances and exits. Split-second identification under time pressure at access points.
