The CAPS Framework

Four capabilities.
The operating system of human performance.

The CAPS framework maps everything your system already collects into four measurable dimensions of operator capability.

CAP-1
Sensemaking
"Can they read the situation correctly across all coverage levels?"

Maps to: Detection Accuracy + Attention/Gaze + camera switching patterns + fixation distribution. Validates: Identification (500 px/m) vs Detection (30 px/m) level awareness

CAP-2
Communication & Coordination
"Can they relay information under pressure?"

Maps to: Alarm handling + marking precision + response protocols + supervisor communication timing

CAP-3
Decision Under Pressure
"Right call, high stakes, short time?"

Maps to: Timeliness + False Positive avoidance + stress-correlated decision quality + HR/EDA at decision points

CAP-4
Recovery & Resilience
"Can they regroup after errors?"

Maps to: Performance after missed detections + distraction recovery time + biometric recovery curves + tunnel vision recovery

Standards Compliance Intelligence

Your standards.
Our intelligence.

Your surveillance framework defines 4 coverage levels. We measure whether your operators actually deliver them under pressure.

Identification Level
500 px/m | 25 fps
"Can the operator identify an unknown person at the entrance?"

Entrances, exits, control rooms. Maps to CAP-1 Sensemaking. CAPS validates whether gaze fixation meets identification-grade attention when it matters most.

Recognition Level
125 px/m | 12 fps
"Can the operator distinguish a known person in the lobby?"

Lobbies, cash areas, corridors. Maps to CAP-1 + CAP-3. Requires sustained attention at recognition-grade density across multiple feeds.

Detection Level
30 px/m | 12 fps
"Can the operator detect movement at the perimeter?"

Perimeters, parking, landscape. Maps to CAP-1 baseline. The minimum standard. When operators fall below this during stress, they are functionally blind.

Vehicle View Level
500 px/m | 25 fps
"Can the operator read the plate at the gate?"

Vehicle entrances and exits. Maps to CAP-3 Decision Under Pressure. Split-second identification under time pressure at access points.
